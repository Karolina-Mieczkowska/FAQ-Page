A language family is a group of languages related to each other through common parental language, called the proto-language. For example language family for the English language is "Germanic", and the descendants of this family are closely related to German, Dutch, Swedish and other Germanic languages. Language families create a family tree as one group forms a group with another. For example, Slavic languages with groups like Germanic or Baltic belong to Indo-European Family. Finno-Ugric languages, though, like Finnish, Hungarian and others belong to the Uralic family.
